I am a chargé de recherches within CNRS, hosted by the LAMFA in Amiens (part of the Université de Picardie Jules Verne), since December 2024. I am a member of the team GAT (Groups, Algebra, Topology).


I have been lucky to be a postdoctoral researcher within the School of Mathematics of the University of Edinburgh working with Ben Davison between 2021 and 2024. From 2021 - 2023, I was funded by an ERC Starting Grant. From 2023 - 2024, I was funded by the Royal Society.

In the Spring of 2024, I visited SLMath (=MSRI) for the thematic programme "Noncommutative Algebraic Geometry".

Between September 2018 and September 2021, I was a PhD student at the Laboratoire de Mathématiques d'Orsay (Université Paris-Saclay), in the team Arithmétique et Géométrie Algébrique, supervised by Olivier Schiffmann. During my PhD, I have been interested in the Hall algebra of quivers, curves and their geometric incarnations: Ringel's construction of the positive part of the quantum group, Lusztig perverse sheaves, cohomological Hall algebras, cuspidal polynomials of quivers...


Research

Research themes

My research is in geometric representation theory. It has something to do with representation theory (understanding algebraic structures by making them act on linear spaces), algebraic geometry (involving moduli spaces of sheaves on curves, surfaces, threefolds), topology (cohomology) and may involve a categorical framework (derived categories and stability conditions, dg categories).

Key-words

Moduli spaces, perverse sheaves, coherent sheaves on curves, Higgs sheaves, cohomological Hall algebras, mixed Hodge modules, Calabi-Yau categories, Donaldson-Thomas theory, reductive groups, quivers, quantum groups.
