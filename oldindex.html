I am a postdoctoral researcher at the University of Edinburgh working with Ben Davison since September 2021. From 2021 - 2023, I was funded by an ERC Starting Grant. From 2023, I am funded by the Royal Society.

My research is in geometric representation theory.

From December 2024, I will be CNRS researcher hosted by the LAMFA in Amiens (France), in the team GAT (Groups, Algebra, Topology).

In the Spring of 2024, I visited SLMath (=MSRI) for the thematic programme "Noncommutative Algebraic Geometry".

Between September 2018 and September 2021, I was a PhD student at the Laboratoire de Mathématiques d'Orsay (Université Paris-Saclay), in the team Arithmétique et Géométrie Algébrique, supervised by Olivier Schiffmann. During my PhD, I have been interested in the Hall algebra of quivers, curves and their geometric incarnations: Ringel's construction of the positive part of the quantum group, Lusztig perverse sheaves, cohomological Hall algebras, cuspidal polynomials of quivers... Now, I am working on the connections between constructible and cohomological Hall algebras.

Key-words

Quivers, quantum groups, perverse sheaves, coherent sheaves on curves, Higgs sheaves, cohomological Hall algebra, mixed Hodge modules, Calabi-Yau categories, Donaldson-Thomas theory.
